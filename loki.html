• Bio
      Loki is the adopted brother and primary antagonist of Thor. He's a god of mischief and trickery, known for his cunning intellect and shape-shifting abilities. Driven by a deep-seated desire for power and recognition, Loki often schemes to take the throne of Asgard, though his motivations are complex, stemming from feelings of abandonment and rivalry with his brother. Despite his villainous tendencies, Loki is a multi-faceted character, occasionally showing vulnerability and even moments of heroism, making him one of Marvel's most intriguing and unpredictable figures.
  • Childhood
    • Bio family
        Loki's childhood is shaped by his complex relationships with both his biological and adoptive families. Born to Laufey, the king of the Frost Giants, Loki was abandoned shortly after birth in a battle between the Frost Giants and Asgardians. He was discovered by Odin, king of Asgard, who adopted him and raised him alongside his biological son, Thor.
    • Adopted family
        Growing up in Asgard, Loki always felt like an outsider, overshadowed by Thor's strength and natural leadership. While Odin raised him as a son, Loki struggled with feelings of inadequacy and a longing for approval. His strained relationship with Thor—who was the favored heir to the throne—deepened his insecurities. Loki eventually discovers his true heritage as a Frost Giant, which fuels his bitterness and sense of betrayal, leading to his desire for power and recognition. His childhood, marked by these tensions, ultimately drives his later actions as a trickster and would-be ruler.
  • Acts
    • Scheming to usurp the throne of Asgard
        In Thor (2011), Loki tries to take control of Asgard after learning he is the adopted son of Odin.
    • The Chitauri Invasion
        In The Avengers (2012), Loki leads an alien invasion of Earth, seeking to conquer it with the help of the Chitauri army.
    • Killing Odin and Taking the Throne
        In Thor: Ragnarok (2017), Loki briefly assumes the throne of Asgard after faking Odin’s death, only for it to be revealed that he’s not fully in control.
    • Turning Against Thanos
        In Avengers: Infinity War (2018), Loki tries to deceive Thanos in an attempt to protect Thor, but is killed for his betrayal.
    • Time-Traveling and Self-Discovery
        In the Loki (2021) TV series, the 2012 version of Loki escapes with the Tesseract, leading to a journey of self-reflection and exploring alternate timelines.
    • Saving all of the timelines
        In the Loki (2023) TV series, Loki helps save the multiverse after discovering the chaos caused by the collapse of the TVA. He confronts the "He Who Remains" variant and, with allies like Sylvie and Mobius, works to prevent the multiverse from falling apart. By the end, Loki takes on a leadership role to stabilize the timelines, showing a more heroic side of his character.
  • Powers
    • Shapeshifting
        Loki can change his appearance and form, assuming different identities and disguises.
    • Illusion Casting
        He can create convincing illusions to deceive others, often making them see things that aren't real.
    • Magic
        Loki is highly skilled in Asgardian magic, using it for a variety of purposes like energy blasts, telekinesis, and teleportation.
    • Superhuman Strength and Durability
        As an Asgardian, Loki possesses enhanced strength and resilience, though not as much as Thor.
    • Regeneration
        Loki can heal from injuries more quickly than ordinary humans, thanks to his Asgardian physiology.
    • Expert Manipulator
        While not a traditional "power," Loki is a master of manipulation and strategy, often using his intellect to outsmart others.
  • Education
      He is highly intelligent, well-read, and skilled in strategy, indicating a sharp mind honed through years of learning in Asgard's libraries and from personal experience. His ability to manipulate people, craft illusions, and understand complex magical and political systems shows that he was trained in more than just physical combat, but also in the arts of deception and leadership. Though Loki's education isn't the focus of his story, it’s clear that he's a product of both Asgardian royal teachings and his own cunning and curiosity, which he uses to further his goals and ambitions.

In the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), Loki, portrayed by Tom Hiddleston, is a complex character known for his wit, cunning, and charisma. He is introduced as the adopted son of Odin, the king of Asgard, and the younger brother of Thor. Initially, Loki struggles with his identity and sense of belonging, especially when he learns of his true heritage as a Frost Giant, the natural enemy of Asgardians. This revelation stirs up resentment, and his feelings of inadequacy fuel his ambition to claim power and prove himself, even if it means betraying his family.

Loki’s character arc is marked by his transformation from villain to antihero, displaying growth that is both gradual and compelling. In *Thor* (2011), he is motivated by envy and a desire for validation, which leads him to attempt to take the throne of Asgard by deceit. In *The Avengers* (2012), he becomes a formidable villain, trying to conquer Earth with the help of the Chitauri army and the Tesseract. However, Loki’s motivations often go beyond simple malice; he is deeply conflicted, driven by an insatiable need to be seen and valued.

As the MCU progresses, Loki’s evolution is brought to life through his relationship with Thor, especially in *Thor: Ragnarok* (2017). Here, we see Loki wrestling with his sense of family loyalty and ultimately choosing to fight alongside his brother to save Asgard. His journey continues in the *Loki* series on Disney+, where a variant version of him, called the "Loki variant," embarks on a quest for self-discovery that forces him to confront his past choices. This exploration of identity, redemption, and free will adds depth to his character and highlights Loki’s intrinsic struggle between his desire for power and his capacity for good.
